Morning Debate Matches: Veccies Dominates, Equal Proves Its Strength

This morning, the class witnessed two intense and engaging debate matches between representatives of the four main parties: Veccies, Maple, Equal, and Pink. These debates were part of a leadership and critical thinking activity that gave each candidate a chance to prove their vision and abilities.

In the first match, the topic was "Online learning at EMC is less effective than in-person classes." Christies, representing the Veccies Party, delivered an outstanding performance that left no doubt about her debating skills. Her arguments were well-organized, backed with clear evidence, and presented with confidence. In contrast, Leon from the Maple Party struggled to keep up, with weaker rebuttals and a lack of clear structure. Christies clearly dominated the stage and strengthened the image of Veccies as a leading party in the race.

The second debate, between Peter of the Equal Party and Doris of the Pink Party, was more balanced and emotionally charged. Doris brought a gentle and empathetic approach, focusing on class harmony and emotional well-being. Peter, on the other hand, used logical reasoning and a calm, structured speaking style.
